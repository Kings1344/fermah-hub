What Fermah is Building: Solving the ZK Bottleneck

Fermah is building a decentralized, two-sided **marketplace** designed to solve the complexity and high cost associated with generating **Zero-Knowledge Proofs (ZKPs)**. While ZKPs are essential for the scalability and privacy of all modern blockchain applications (ZK rollups, zkEVMs, and ZK bridges), the current ecosystem faces severe bottlenecks:

  • **High Hardware Cost:** Proof generation demands massive computational power, primarily from specialized, expensive hardware (high-end GPUs, ASICs).
  • **Orchestration Complexity:** Projects must maintain complex, localized infrastructure to queue and manage proving jobs, leading to centralized points of failure.
  • **Inconsistent Speed:** Latency varies wildly based on available resources and proof complexity, impacting dApp user experience.

Fermah's mission is to abstract away this infrastructure complexity, providing a **Universal Proof Generation Layer** that makes ZKPs **cheap, fast, and reliably accessible** for any developer. Critically, Fermah ensures **credible neutrality** by being architected to support *all* major proof systems‚Äîincluding zkVMs, zkEVMs, Groth16, PLONK, and STARKs‚Äîmaking it the end-game infrastructure for ZK computation.

The Proof Generation Marketplace

Seekers (Demand)

These are the users and projects (ZK rollups, DeFi protocols, privacy apps) that **request** proofs. They submit the computation they need verified without having to worry about the underlying hardware or proving process.

Matchmaker (Orchestrator)

The core routing layer that intelligently connects Seekers' proof requests to the most optimal **Prover Node** based on speed, cost, and machine capability. It ensures efficient resource allocation across the network.

Prover Nodes (Supply)

The decentralized infrastructure providers who offer computational power (GPUs, FPGAs) to generate the proofs. Initially bootstrapped by **EigenLayer Operators** for enhanced security.
